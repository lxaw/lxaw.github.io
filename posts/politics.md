As machines inevitably become smarter and better, the question remains as to what we should do. Should we do anything?

In this way, the emergence of super intelligence brings us back to the philosophies. Technical ability as you may know will slowly be replaced by the ability to manage agents and craft good rules for them.

Ultimately I think it will all boil down to individual philosophies and moral standards. That is the heart of prompting; how can we communicate the ideas we want to build accurately and ensure that the agents we work with follow our rules and regulations.

Surely this too will be automated such that we don't need to be so careful. Later we may suffer the consequences of such jumps.

This is all to say, if we have systems that are better than us in essentially every technical way, and likely will be better in non-technical ways as well, then what should we do today?

Of course there is no answer, but that's boring. So let's set up some structure to the argument so we can try to derive something.

As an assumption, we want humanity to thrive and continue, while reigning in the power of AI tools.

To be honest, I don't think we will really be able to ever understand if we are truly reigning in these tools. They will just be too intelligent for our understanding. We should certainly have people working on this topic, but ultimately I think there are limits to what we can really accomplish here, although we are still just scratching the surface. Maybe we will be able to integrate with AI and augment our intelligence to understand such things, but for now I will not make this assumption.

So then we should think about ourselves, as this is 1) something we can feasibly do and 2) may be the more likely cause of any serious issues.

So this motivates a study of politics, and naturally, history.
